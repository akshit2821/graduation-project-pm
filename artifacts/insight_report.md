# Insight report

**Corpus:** 629 posts
**Coverage:** 100.0% themed
**Target segment:** Indian urban listeners aged 22–35 who listen 5+ hours/week, get ≥60% of listening from saved playlists or liked songs, and rarely engage with Discover Weekly meaningfully

## Why do users struggle to discover new music?
*Discovery pain comes from choice overload, cluttered UI, and libraries too large to browse—not only bad picks.*

- **Hard to find new music**: 306 posts (48.6%), avg rating 4.03
- **Library too large to explore**: 15 posts (2.4%), avg rating 3.0
- **UI blocks exploration**: 13 posts (2.1%), avg rating 2.92
- *Combined signal:* 334 posts (53.1% of themed corpus)

## What are the most common frustrations with recommendations?
*Recommendation frustration includes stale or irrelevant picks, but also ads and UI changes that interrupt exploration.*

- **Recommendation products miss**: 25 posts (4.0%), avg rating 3.92
- **Free tier limits exploration**: 209 posts (33.2%), avg rating 2.34
- **UI blocks exploration**: 13 posts (2.1%), avg rating 2.92
- *Combined signal:* 247 posts (39.3% of themed corpus)

## What listening behaviors are users trying to achieve?
*Users describe comfort loops, shared discovery with friends, and balancing podcasts vs music—not always seeking novelty.*

- **Same content on loop**: 30 posts (4.8%), avg rating 2.09
- **Wants shared discovery**: 7 posts (1.1%), avg rating 1.67
- **Podcasts crowd out music**: 24 posts (3.8%), avg rating 3.31
- *Combined signal:* 61 posts (9.7% of themed corpus)

## What causes users to repeatedly listen to the same content?
*Repeat listening is driven by habit, ad friction when browsing, oversized libraries, and decision fatigue when choosing something new.*

- **Same content on loop**: 30 posts (4.8%), avg rating 2.09

- **Library too large to explore**: 15 posts (2.4%), avg rating 3.0
- **Hard to find new music**: 306 posts (48.6%), avg rating 4.03
- *Combined signal:* 560 posts (89.0% of themed corpus)
